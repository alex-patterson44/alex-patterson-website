Core Value Exploration

Through my leadership and team experiences at Eller, I've explored how my values, including connection, growth, and purpose, shape my approach in professional settings. My goal was to understand how closely my self-perception aligned with how others see me in professional settings.

I reflected on my Self-Perception Inventory, Professional Reputation Survey, and 360 evaluations through my BCOM 314 class to identify overlaps between my identity and reputation. I described myself as a growth-oriented, social, and hard-working individual. These traits reflect my commitment to building relationships, learning, and achieving meaningful results.

My peers reinforced these qualities, often describing me as leaderlike, confident, and motivated. Their feedback confirmed that I naturally demonstrate connection through collaboration, growth through curiosity, and purpose through follow-through and impact on big group projects. I learned that my core values are not only visible to others but also shape my professional reputation. Moving forward, I plan to continually refine my communication and leadership skills to ensure that how I connect, grow, and contribute always reflects the professional I strive to be.

Behavioral Communication Research Findings

Screenshot 2025-11-04 at 3.33.13 PM.png

My communication research findings highlight the difference between how I perceive myself and how others experience me. When I first completed my Self-Perception Inventory, I landed in the Amiable and Analytical quadrants. This result surprised me, as I have often been categorized as a Driver or Expressive type. I view myself as someone who demonstrates warmth and thoughtfulness in communication, and my self-perception results reinforced this tendency toward empathy and reflection.

However, my Behavioral Communication Style Profile, which gathered feedback from teammates, peers, and a former supervisor, revealed a different perspective, placing me firmly in the Expressive quadrant. Respondents described me as hardworking, confident, and leaderlike, highlighting how my ideal professional reputation is perceived by others.

From these insights, I learned that while my confidence and drive are clear strengths, I can continue to grow by delegating more effectively and creating more space for others to contribute toward shared goals. This feedback has deepened my understanding of effective leadership, and recognizing this has helped me refine my communication style by balancing my expressive energy with greater intention and awareness in collaborative settings.

Team Culture Findings

Throughout our team projects this semester, I served as both a motivator and organizer, helping shape a team culture centered on accountability, open communication, and respect. Our team relied heavily on consistent collaboration to manage multiple deliverables and stay aligned on shared goals. My primary goal was to maintain structure and engagement while fostering a positive and inclusive team culture where every member felt heard, valued, and empowered to contribute.

I consistently led by example, preparing thoroughly for meetings, initiating discussions, and ensuring tasks were clearly divided. My focus on communication and encouragement helped sustain momentum, especially during high-pressure periods. At the same time, I worked on stepping back to let others lead at moments, encouraging shared ownership. Feedback from my peers highlighted my leadership, organization, and reliability as key drivers of our team's success.

Identity · Brand · Reputation

During class debriefs and discussions with my Professional Persona partners, I've gained deeper insight into how my warmth and competence scores from Radian help me bridge the gap between my intended identity and my actual reputation. These reflections have helped me understand how my presence, communication style, and leadership tendencies influence how others perceive me.

Through feedback from my peers and teammates, I've learned that I'm often perceived as a confident and capable leader, someone who takes initiative, and ensures tasks are completed. At the same time, I've also discovered that my drive for excellence can sometimes make me appear most comfortable when I'm in control.

Recognizing this has been an essential step in my growth as a leader. I'm learning to delegate more effectively, trust others' abilities, and create space for teammates to showcase their strengths. This balance not only strengthens team dynamics but also leads to more collaborative and creative outcomes. These experiences continue to help me align my personal identity with my professional reputation, fostering authentic leadership built on both confidence and connection.

My growth mindset is grounded in my core values of connection, purpose, and growth. I view every experience, whether it's constructive feedback, facing a challenge, or achieving success, as an opportunity to learn and grow. One of my primary areas for growth is enabling others to contribute more fully in team settings. I've learned that great leadership isn't just about taking initiative, but also about creating space for others to share their ideas and strengths.

As I move through the remainder of this semester and participate in the case competition for my BCOM 314 course, I plan to intentionally implement this by encouraging more open conversations, delegating key tasks, and trusting my teammates to take ownership of their contributions. By doing so, I'll continue to strengthen collaboration, elevate team performance, and grow into a more balanced and empowering leader.
