My Story

I came to the University of Arizona on a military scholarship through ROTC, where I initially pursued Mechanical and then Civil Engineering. As a first-generation college student, stepping into higher education without a family roadmap taught me how to be resourceful, disciplined, and intentional from the start. ROTC reinforced those values by demanding accountability, composure under pressure, and leadership through action.

After immersing myself in the military path, I made the courageous decision to step back and realign with what truly drives me. I found my fiancee, a renewed sense of direction, and a calling that felt undeniably right: accounting. That decision was not a setback, but one of the most intentional and self-aware choices I have made. As a first-generation student, it also represented my commitment to creating opportunity for myself and my family through long-term growth and purpose-driven work.

I now study accounting at the Eller College of Management, where I discovered a genuine passion for tax, including the strategy, complexity, and real impact it has on people and organizations. I am actively preparing for a career in tax where I can combine analytical thinking, leadership, and clear communication to deliver results that matter.

Professional Background

I am an Accounting student at the Eller College of Management with hands-on experience in collaborative coursework, professional presentations, and business writing. My path to college was not a straightforward one — coming from a background with little prior exposure to higher education, I took initiative by enrolling in college preparatory programs and actively seeking out leadership opportunities whenever they presented themselves.

My journey to college was further shaped by my commitment to service through ROTC, an experience that reinforced the discipline, accountability, and leadership principles I have carried throughout my life. Having grown up as the oldest of four siblings, I developed a natural sense of responsibility and leadership from an early age — qualities that have followed me into every professional and academic setting.

My background includes several years of work experience that have sharpened my ability to communicate effectively across diverse audiences, contribute meaningfully to team-driven projects, and deliver work that produces real, measurable outcomes.
