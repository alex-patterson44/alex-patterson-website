The moments that shaped how I work.

This page is built from the reflection prompts in your assignment. It focuses on what you learned from teamwork, project management, presentations, difficult conversations, and upper-division coursework.

Teamwork

What I learned

I learned that strong teams are not built by talking the most. They are built by listening, clarifying roles, and making space for different working styles. I became better at communicating deadlines, checking understanding, and keeping the group moving without taking over.

Managing projects in three courses

What changed

Managing multiple projects taught me to break large tasks into milestones and to work backward from the due date. I now pay attention to sequencing, accountability, and the fact that good planning lowers stress for the whole team.

Preparing for presentations

My approach

Preparation is no longer just about knowing the material. I now think about audience, pacing, visuals, and the questions people may ask afterward. That shift has made my presentations more focused and more confident.

Engaging in difficult conversations

Professional growth

Hard conversations require calm language, respect, and a clear goal. I have learned to address problems early, keep the conversation centered on the issue instead of the person, and stay open to feedback even when the discussion is uncomfortable.

Upper-division coursework

Academic development

Upper-division work pushed me to be more independent and more accountable. I became more comfortable with ambiguity, more willing to revise my thinking, and more aware that polished work usually comes from several rounds of improvement.

What drives me

Identity and motivation

I want to be known as someone who can help a team stay organized, communicate clearly, and produce work people can rely on. I am proud when my work makes other people’s work easier.
